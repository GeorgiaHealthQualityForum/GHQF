Tbilisi Medical Academy


Tbilisi Medical Academy was founded in 1992 by notable Georgian physician and pediatric surgeon Petre Shotadze. From its humble beginnings in 1992, more than 31 years TMA is one of the pioneers of medical education in Georgia and remains to be one of the most competitive and dynamically developing institutions.  Since its foundation, TMA has grown to more than 2500 graduates who work successfully in leading Georgian and International Hospitals, Universities, and Scientific Centres.

TMA, as a student-oriented higher education institution, primarily seeks to teach and develop national and international healthcare professionals in an innovative manner by using evidence-based knowledge, whilst practically benefiting the community in Georgia through research-based outreach activities.

The main goal of the Academy is to elaborate independent and critical thinking, scientific reasoning abilities, and reasonable judgments among students, to support their development into highly qualified specialists.

Latest Authorization

In September 2024, the National Center For Educational Quality Enhancement, Georgian, and international expert committee granted TMA unconditional authorization for 6 years. This result reflects the commitment of everyone involved and highlights the positive steps we’ve taken as a university. It was a team effort, and this recognition marks an important milestone in the continued journey forward.

WFME

TMA’s educational programs “Medicine” and ''Medicine In English'' are successfully granted full accreditation from the World Federation of Medical Education (WFME), which provides TMA graduates with opportunities for further education and employment in all parts of the world.
